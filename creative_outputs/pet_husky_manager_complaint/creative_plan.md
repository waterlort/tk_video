## Content Concept And Caveat

Miso, a single recurring husky, reacts to dinner being three minutes late as if filing a workplace complaint against the owner. The comedy comes from treating a normal husky attitude moment as a mock manager escalation, using a simple complaint-ticket overlay.

Use this as a scout-informed creative demo only. Round 02 supports `#husky` as a clearer breed-personality direction than broad cute tags, but the evidence is caption-backed, incomplete, and not enough to call this a proven viral template.

## Observed / Provided Features

- Topic: pet / husky breed-personality comedy.
- Required persistent subject: one dog only, `dog_01` / `Miso`.
- Required identity anchor: adult gray-and-white Siberian husky, symmetrical gray mask, narrow white blaze, left icy-blue eye, right amber-brown eye, red collar, small round silver tag.
- Setting: small clean apartment kitchen/living-room corner with light oak floor, off-white wall, white lower cabinet, stainless steel bowl on gray mat, one blue chew toy.

## Interpretation

### Active Category

- Primary: `entertainment wrapper`
- Secondary: `story and identity`
- Context tags: breed-personality comedy, future product/affiliate fit, visual identity lock

The viewer promise is not "look at a cute dog." It is "this exact husky is acting like a tiny angry manager," with behavior proof in the first three seconds.

### Audience Motive

- Recognition: husky owners know this argument/stomp behavior.
- Tagging: viewers can tag a pet owner whose dog does the same thing.
- Affectionate frustration: the owner is mildly "in trouble" without making the dog distressed.
- Comment play: viewers can write their own pet's complaint form.

### Viewer Tension

"What exactly did Miso file a complaint about, and how will the owner settle the case?"

The open loop is tiny but clear: the dog has a grievance, the ticket explains it, and the payoff is whether the complaint is resolved.

### Hook Type

- Hyper-specific POV comedy.
- Breed stereotype / behavior proof.
- Specific physical behavior proof: paw stomp or husky talking expression.

### First-Three-Second Logic

0.0-1.1s:
- Show Miso beside the empty bowl at dog height.
- Text: `Dinner was 3 minutes late.`

1.1-2.6s:
- Cut immediately to paw lift or stomp.
- Text: `Complaint filed.`
- Add a small stamp SFX.

2.6-3.0s:
- Miso looks toward camera with husky-talking mouth shape.
- Keep the red collar and mismatched eyes visible if possible.

By second 3, the viewer should understand the situation, the breed-specific behavior, and the mock complaint device.

### Script Structure

- `POV setup -> enactment -> escalation`
- `Claim -> demonstration -> implication`
- Micro-arc: late dinner -> paw stomp complaint -> mock damages -> owner proof -> food settlement -> comment question.

### Filming Method

- Vertical 9:16 UGC-style smartphone footage.
- Low dog-height camera facing the bowl area.
- Owner appears only as hands/legs in neutral gray sleeves.
- Natural room sound can carry the husky vocal and bowl/food details.
- Overlay is a simple translucent complaint ticket, not TikTok UI and not a real legal/medical document.

### Editing Rhythm

- Fast first three seconds: 1.0-1.5 second beats.
- Middle: 2.0-3.0 second comedy beats.
- End: 2.0-2.5 second payoff and loopable stare.
- Use straight cuts, not heavy transitions.
- Keep text large, short, and inside title-safe areas.

### Music / Audio Role

- Primary: light deadpan comedy bed, pizzicato, dry marimba, or plucky muted bass at low volume.
- Secondary: source audio for paw stomp, husky talking, scoop/food pour, bowl sounds.
- SFX: tiny stamp on `Complaint filed`, paper rustle for ticket overlay, tiny ding on `Case settled`.
- Avoid chaotic meme audio, dramatic rescue music, barking loops, and product-ad music beds.

## Risk Notes

- Identity drift is blocking: different husky markings, same-color eyes, missing red collar, extra dogs, or changed room geography invalidates the asset.
- Animal welfare: do not stage hunger, distress, punishment, forced vocalization, or food withholding. The "3 minutes late" joke must stay light.
- Legal/medical framing: the complaint ticket is playful only. Avoid official document language, diagnoses, injury claims, or punitive owner behavior.
- Performance overclaim: do not call the plan viral, proven, or durable. It is a scout-informed demo using incomplete evidence.
- Text legibility: generated tools often garble overlay text. If text is unreliable, generate clean footage without embedded text and add overlays in editing.
- Comedy specificity: if the stomp/vocal behavior is missing, the video becomes generic caption comedy and should be reworked before production.
