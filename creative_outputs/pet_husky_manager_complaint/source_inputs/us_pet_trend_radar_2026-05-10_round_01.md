## Cross-category scout snapshot

Source: AdCreate, US, 2026-05-03 to 2026-05-09.

Overall high-volume trends include `#prom`, `#prom2026`, `#promdress`, `#promtransition`, `#stagecoach`, sports/player tags, and news/entertainment tags. For pets this suggests testing seasonal/event translation carefully, for example:

- pet outfit transformation tied to prom season,

- no direct copy of unrelated event videos.

These are adaptation leads only, not pet-vertical claims.

## Format leads

1. Breed/community specificity may be stronger than generic pet tags.
   - `#pibble`, `#husky`, `#doberman`, `#bulldog`, and `#pomeranian` imply subcommunities with recognizable traits and comment identities.
   - Next validation: collect real sample videos under each tag and compare comment depth, not just views.

2. Non-dog/cat niches are visible.
   - `#backyardchickens`, `#goatsoftiktok`, and `#birdwatching` suggest hobby/community surfaces that could produce repeatable series.
   - Next validation: determine whether these are pets, homestead lifestyle, nature observation, or novelty clips.

3. AI animal videos are a possible format lead but also a visual-consistency risk.
   - Secondary reporting points to absurd but realistic AI cat clips as a shareable pattern.
   - For tk_video, this increases the need for identity lock: if the same cat/dog changes between shots, the concept fails.

4. TikTok 2026 platform guidance favors comment/community/search loops.
   - Pet content should invite pet-owner identity comments, not just passive likes.
   - Candidate prompts: "what would your dog score this?", "which breed does this?", "does your cat do this too?", "rate the setup", "is this normal?"

## Risk notes

- Do not use `#servicedog` casually. It can involve disability, access rights, training claims, and harassment risk.
- Do not over-index on broad cute tags. They are large but may be saturated and weak for prediction.
- Do not treat third-party mirrors as enough evidence for durable claims.
- Do not generate new visual examples until identity-lock and sample evidence gates are satisfied.
