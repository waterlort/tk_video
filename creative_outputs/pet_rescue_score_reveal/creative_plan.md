## Observed / Provided Design Features

- US-facing pet concept for a general traffic account.
- Later conversion may involve pet products or affiliate paths, but v1 should prioritize traffic, comments, follows, and trust.
- Core situation: cautious newly adopted dog "rates" the home with a playful comfort score overlay.
- Score rises only after calm owner actions: quiet corner, blanket, water, favorite toy, slow hand/no grabbing, routine.
- Required safety boundaries: no medical diagnosis, no animal distress, no unsafe handling, no guaranteed rescue transformation.
- First version should not hard sell.
- Requested length: 15-20 seconds.

## Interpretation

### Content Category

- Primary: `story and identity`
- Secondary: `product-adjacent demo` and light `practical utility`
- Context tags: `scan/score reveal`, `future product/affiliate fit`

The viewer promise is not "buy this item." It is an emotional micro-story: "we learned what made this newly adopted dog feel safe." The score overlay is a hook mechanism, not the top-level category.

### Audience Motive

- Emotional payoff: seeing a cautious pet choose safety.
- Curiosity: what actions move the comfort score from 2/10 to 9/10?
- Recognition: pet owners compare their own routines.
- Problem relief: first-week pet settling, calm-home setup.
- Later buying confidence: products can be shown inside a trusted routine after the account earns attention.

### Viewer Tension

"What changed the dog from cautious at the doorway to choosing a safe corner?"

The score overlay keeps a small open loop alive. Each owner action should visibly move either the dog or the score so the viewer has a reason to stay until the 9/10 reveal.

### Hook Type

- `Score/scan reveal`
- Quiet contradiction: the owner is trying less, but the score rises.
- Soft time jump: Day 1 score versus nighttime score.

### First 3 Seconds

0.0-1.0s:
- Dog at doorway or edge of room, cautious but calm.
- On-screen text: `Day 1: he rated our home 2/10`
- No product, no forced interaction.

1.0-2.0s:
- Playful translucent scanner/score overlay locks onto the dog or corner.
- On-screen text: `comfort scan: starting low`
- Tiny UI beep.

2.0-3.0s:
- First owner action enters frame: blanket placed in a quiet corner, owner steps back.
- Score ticks to `3/10`.
- The dog does not need to approach yet; the visible change is the owner choosing calm instead of pressure.

### Script Structure

- `Before -> process -> after`
- `Subject/problem -> scan/score -> reveal/implication`

### Filming Method

- Vertical UGC-style phone footage.
- Dog-eye-level shots, natural apartment light, cozy but not staged.
- Voiceover with b-roll or text-led montage.
- Optional app/scan overlay added in edit.
- Avoid face-first creator framing unless a hand/foot helps humanize the routine.

### Editing Rhythm

- Caption-led progressive reveal.
- 0.7-1.6 seconds per shot.
- Score changes every 2-4 seconds.
- Soft match cut from cautious doorway to dog choosing the corner.
- Loop ending: final question visually returns to the first doorway/corner framing.

### Music / Audio Role

- Primary: emotional framing with soft piano, warm acoustic, or gentle lo-fi bed.
- Secondary: UI beep/chime on score changes.
- Voiceover should be calm, not dramatic.
- Avoid sad rescue music, medical-sounding scanner audio, or meme audio that fights the tenderness.

## Editing Directions

- Aspect ratio: 9:16.
- Length: 18 seconds.
- Cut on score changes, not just beat changes.
- Keep subtitles on every voiceover line.
- Score overlay should look playful and lightweight:
  - small rounded label near dog/corner
  - `comfort score`
  - score increments: `2/10`, `3/10`, `4/10`, `6/10`, `9/10`
  - use soft green or warm yellow, not clinical blue.
- Use one tiny UI beep per score change.
- Keep product items unbranded and secondary.
- Final frame should last at least 1.2 seconds so viewers can answer the CTA.
