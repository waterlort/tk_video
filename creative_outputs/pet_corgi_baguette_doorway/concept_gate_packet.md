## Core Concept

Mochi, a small corgi mix, insists on carrying an oversized plush baguette toy through a narrow doorway. The toy is too wide, so both ends hit the doorframe in the first 2 seconds. Mochi refuses to drop it, backs up, tilts it diagonally, squeezes through, and looks back like the doorway was the problem.

## Subject Identity

Use `subject_identity_bible.md` before any static image, frame extraction, or motion prompt work.

Required persistent elements:

- one small tan-and-white adult corgi mix named Mochi;
- aqua collar with a small round brass tag;
- white lightning-shaped blaze on the forehead;
- left ear with a folded tip, right ear upright;
- oversized plush baguette toy, not real food and not branded;
- narrow white interior doorway, light oak floor, sage mat nearby.

Reject any output where Mochi becomes a different dog, the plush toy becomes real bread, the collar changes color, the doorway scale changes enough to remove the obstacle, or the dog appears distressed.

## Audio And Text

- Audio role: source audio first; no voiceover required.
- Must capture: soft doorway thump, tiny paw shuffle, optional toy squeak, gentle room tone.
- Music: optional very low comedic bed only if it does not cover the thump.
- Captions: short, readable, and behavior-framing only. Text must not explain the whole joke.
- Avoid: loud meme audio, sad rescue music, product-ad style, or over-humanized narration.

## Risk Review

- Animal welfare: safe if Mochi is not pulled, scared, trapped, or forced. The dog should remain playful and calm.
- Identity drift: high production risk. Require subject bible, first-frame approval, and reviewer pass.
- Prop confusion: medium risk. The baguette must read as a plush toy, not real bread or a chewable unsafe object.
- Product risk: low now. Do not mention brand, price, durability, or shop link.
- Copyright/music: low if using source audio or licensed-safe background.
- Overclaim risk: low if kept as comedy test, not as a pet-training or product claim.

## Metric Hypothesis

Primary test metric:

- retention through the first doorway hit at 2 seconds;
- rewatch rate around the diagonal workaround;
- shares/tags from pet owners;
- comments naming objects pets tried to carry.

Success signal:

- viewers understand the physical conflict without explanation and comment with their own pet behavior.

Failure signals:

- the toy/doorway scale is not clear;
- the dog looks distressed or forced;
- the diagonal workaround takes too long;
- text carries the joke instead of action;
- generated frames or motion change Mochi's identity.
