# Subject identity bible: Mochi

## Identity Lock

- Subject ID: `dog_01_mochi`
- Name: Mochi
- Species/type: small adult corgi mix
- Age feel: young adult, energetic but calm
- Size: compact, short-legged, about 22-26 lb
- Body shape: low, sturdy corgi-like build; short legs; slightly long body; fluffy but not oversized coat
- Face: rounded corgi face, alert expression, dark brown eyes, black nose
- Ears: right ear upright; left ear mostly upright with a folded tip
- Coat pattern: warm tan head, ears, back, and sides; white chest, belly, muzzle, and front paws
- Unique marking: narrow white lightning-shaped blaze from forehead down to muzzle
- Collar: matte aqua-blue fabric collar with a small round brass tag, no logo
- Behavior identity: stubborn toy-carrier; proud, playful, not fearful

## Required Prop

- Prop ID: `toy_01_plush_baguette`
- Type: oversized plush baguette dog toy
- Size: about 28-30 inches long; visibly wider than the doorway when held horizontally
- Look: soft pale-golden fabric, three darker stitched diagonal "crust" marks, rounded plush ends
- Safety: clearly plush, not real bread, not hard, not splintering, no stuffing spilling out
- Branding: none

## Required Location

- Location ID: `doorway_01_hallway`
- Narrow white interior doorway with simple trim
- Light oak floor
- Small sage-green floor mat near the doorway
- Warm natural apartment light
- No visible logos, unsafe clutter, stairs, wires, food bowls, or product packaging

## Continuity Rules

- Mochi must stay the same dog in every frame and motion shot.
- Keep the aqua collar and brass tag visible whenever the neck is visible.
- Keep the right ear upright and left ear folded at the tip.
- Keep the white blaze, white muzzle, white chest, and white front paws consistent.
- Keep `toy_01_plush_baguette` the same length, color, and soft plush material.
- Keep the doorway narrow enough that the toy obviously hits both sides when horizontal.
- The toy can tilt diagonally for the payoff, but it must not shrink.

## Frame 01 Reference Requirement

Before generating a sequence, approve a first frame showing:

- Mochi centered or entering from room side;
- plush baguette held horizontally in mouth;
- both toy ends close to or touching the doorway trim;
- aqua collar visible;
- clear white blaze and ear asymmetry.

Use the approved Frame 01 as the character/prop reference for all later frames when the tool supports references.

## Rejection Criteria

Reject generated output if:

- Mochi becomes another breed, puppy, different color, or different size;
- collar changes color or disappears in close shots;
- white blaze, ear asymmetry, or coat pattern changes;
- the toy becomes real bread, a stick, a bone, or a branded product;
- the toy changes length enough that the doorway obstacle disappears;
- the dog appears trapped, frightened, injured, dragged, or forced by a human;
- the setting changes to a store, outdoor scene, kennel, or unsafe cluttered room;
- text overlays cover the physical doorway hit.
