# Proposed Creator-Persona Claims - 2026-05-18

These are candidate/proposed claims from ULT-313. They require TK Knowledge Classifier review, manual video verification, and mixed-outcome counterexamples before TK Knowledge Modifier promotes anything durable.

## candidate_claim_creator_persona_2026_05_18_001

Claim: A faceless product-adjacent TikTok account should anchor its persona in a repeatable testing role rather than in lifestyle identity.

Scope: New or low-authority TikTok accounts in home, pet product, and auto accessory categories.

Applies when: The account cannot show a real human face and needs trust through proof, not personal celebrity.

Does not apply when: The account has an established human creator, celebrity pet, professional credentials, or owned media audience that already supplies trust.

Supporting cases: `case_2026_05_18_creator_001_home_tiktok_organization_persona`, `002`, `007`, `008`.

Counterexamples: needs face-led creator controls and failed faceless product accounts.

Observed metrics: public proxy follower/view metrics only; no conversion data.

Confidence: low-medium.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.

Next validation test: Run the 12-video batch with a consistent test-bench identity and compare comments for trust/spam perception.

## candidate_claim_creator_persona_2026_05_18_002

Claim: Home and auto product demos should usually lead with use case, result, or friction proof before product explanation.

Scope: Commerce-adjacent TikTok videos under 8-25 seconds.

Applies when: The product is ordinary-looking and needs context before viewers care.

Does not apply when: The object is visually strange enough to carry curiosity by itself or the content is a ranking/debate video.

Supporting cases: `case_2026_05_18_creator_002_home_tiktok_local_product_rows`, `007`.

Counterexamples: needs product-first winners and failed result-first controls.

Observed metrics: local public proxies for home and auto rows; no retention or sales.

Confidence: low-medium.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.

Next validation test: For one home product and one car accessory, test problem-first, result-first, and product-first openings.

## candidate_claim_creator_persona_2026_05_18_003

Claim: Pet content transfers best when it has a fixed subject identity or one clear animal behavior/care mechanism, not broad pet cuteness.

Scope: TikTok pet content and pet product demos for new accounts.

Applies when: The video can show a named pet, a real behavior, a grooming/care transformation, or one bench-tested pet product benefit.

Does not apply when: The source is AI pet-story content, repost-like compilation, or generic cute footage without originality/identity.

Supporting cases: `case_2026_05_18_creator_004_pet_tiktok_behavior_proof`, `005`, `006`.

Counterexamples: broad cute-pet accounts with verified repeated performance; weak fixed-subject examples.

Observed metrics: local public TikTok proxies and secondary follower rankings only.

Confidence: low-medium.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.

Next validation test: Compare one real-behavior pet row, one product-bench row, and one generic cute row after manual verification.

## candidate_claim_creator_persona_2026_05_18_004

Claim: Instagram and Facebook follower size should not be treated as TikTok-native evidence without current video-level proof.

Scope: Cross-platform creator radar and account strategy.

Applies when: A creator/account is identified from secondary rankings or non-TikTok platforms.

Does not apply when: The same account has current manually verified TikTok video evidence with comparable content mechanics.

Supporting cases: `case_2026_05_18_creator_003_home_instagram_authority`, `005`, `009`.

Counterexamples: same-format creators with strong cross-post performance across verified platform videos.

Observed metrics: secondary follower metrics only.

Confidence: medium as process guardrail.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.

Next validation test: For 10 ranked Instagram/Facebook creators, manually sample recent TikTok/Reels/Shorts and compare first-3-second mechanics and public engagement proxies.

## candidate_claim_creator_persona_2026_05_18_005

Claim: Auto accessory content needs a higher compliance gate than home gadgets because fitment, safety, repair, and road-use claims can mislead.

Scope: Auto accessory, car care, and auto-parts short videos.

Applies when: The product touches vehicle operation, visibility, tire pressure, electrical systems, brakes, engine, steering, suspension, driver behavior, or compatibility.

Does not apply when: The product is clearly decorative, storage-only, or cleaning-only and no safety/fitment claim is made.

Supporting cases: `case_2026_05_18_creator_007_auto_tiktok_fast_growth_and_local_rows`, `008`, `010`.

Counterexamples: needs low-risk auto accessory rows with clean policy history and high conversion.

Observed metrics: public proxies only.

Confidence: medium as process guardrail, low as performance claim.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.

Next validation test: Have Policy And Brand Safety Reviewer review all auto test scripts before filming; track comment objections and policy flags.

Counterexamples: manually verified route rows not yet available in this batch.

Observed metrics: public proxies from local JSON.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-18.
