## Scope And Leakage Check

This was not a blind prediction score. The reviewed sample set was the project knowledge/workflow surface:

- project brief and KB design;
- taxonomy, changelog, root memory, and prior judge gates;
- 2026-05-16 cross-vertical trend radar, case packet, playbook, and proposed claims;
- two pet creative final packages;
- prior candidate-002 blind-pilot setup and pet evidence gates.

Outcome metrics were visible inside the scout case packets and creative context. That is acceptable for this review because the task was process audit, not prediction accuracy. It would be invalid to score directional accuracy, hot-video recall, calibration, or false positives from these materials.

Leakage verdict: pass for process-review use, fail for prediction-scoring use. The KB repeatedly labels metric-exposed public-search data as scout input and blocks explainer scoring until sealed manifests, scrubbed packets, and hidden outcomes exist.

## Aggregate Verdict

The project has built a coherent TikTok/TK knowledge workflow and enough low-to-medium confidence creative scaffolding to generate the next concept as a constrained hypothesis. It is not ready to claim predictive power, durable virality relations, or production-ready video performance.

Ready state:

- Ready for next concept brief: conditional yes.
- Ready for storyboard/static/motion generation: only after concept gate, fixed subject identity bible, and visual proof plan.
- Ready for actual viral-test video production: not yet; final assets still need manual review, identity continuity, policy/conversion review, and a test plan.
- Ready for blind prediction scoring: no.
- Ready for durable claim promotion: no, except the already-supported process guardrail that public tag/discover routes are unsafe as category evidence without manual verification.

The smallest next step is not another broad research pass. It is one tightly gated concept packet: one viewer job, one visible first-2-second proof, one dominant mechanism, one fixed subject identity, one shot sequence, one audio role, one risk review, and one metric hypothesis.

## Weaknesses And Risks

- Manual playback is still the major bottleneck. Without first-3-second notes, shot sequence, audio role, comments, source originality, and true duration, many rows are only caption-and-metric hypotheses.
- The project remains top-heavy with high-performing public examples and thin on cold/ordinary controls.
- Search routes are noisy enough that public tag/category matches cannot support category claims by themselves.
- The prediction loop is structurally designed but not executed: no completed candidate-002 sample, no scrubbed packets, no locked predictions, no unblind score.
- Pet evidence is improved but still under-verified. Broad pet topic, cute tags, AI pet-story rows, and real-pet rows must remain separated.
- Production-chain identity drift remains a severe risk for generated animal content. A strong concept still fails if the generated subject changes across frames or motion.
- Workflow fragmentation risk is moderate: many artifacts exist, but the next concept must use one explicit gate packet so the cg agents do not each infer missing constraints differently.
- Policy/conversion risk is currently controlled by restraint, but product, money, side-hustle, safety, raw-feeding, and pet-health-adjacent ideas need separate review before publishing.

## Anti-Overfit Notes

- Do not optimize around one pet concept, one public-search row, one creator, one sound, one tag, or one visible metric.
- Keep cold, ordinary, failed, off-topic, AI-generated, and rejected-production rows as evidence.
- Do not confuse a concept gate with a performance claim.
- Do not treat identity-lock success as virality evidence; it is production readiness only.
- Do not let cg agents inherit missing evidence implicitly. Every next handoff needs explicit gates and limits.
- Require repeated evidence before changing taxonomy or relation claims, except for severe process bugs such as leakage or subject identity drift.

## What To Keep, Change, Deprecate

Keep:

- the classifier/modifier/explainer/judge separation;
- candidate-002 leakage and collection design;
- cross-vertical foundation playbook as concept preflight;
- pet specialness gate, pet idea quality axes, and visual identity lock protocol;
- search-noise and cold-control preservation;
- current restraint against durable claim promotion.

Change:

- Require a one-page concept gate packet before any next generated video concept advances to cg-static-image or cg-motion.
- Add mandatory manual verification fields to any case eligible for classifier confidence: first 3 seconds, actual shot sequence, audio role, comment examples, originality/repost status, and duration.
- Build the next evidence pass around mixed outcomes, not only viral candidates.
- Keep an explainer-safe knowledge snapshot separate from judge-only metric surfaces.
- Add a final production QA step that checks identity continuity, visible mechanism proof, policy risk, and metric hypothesis before publishing.

Deprecate:

- broad tag heat as strategy evidence;
- "pet topic" or "cute pet" as sufficient idea quality;
- `normal pet + text overlay` as a production-ready pet concept;
- any generated multi-shot pet asset without identity bible and reviewer pass;
- any relation/performance/conversion claim promoted from metric-exposed scout rows alone.

No durable-claim promotions are recommended from this review.

## Before Actual Viral-Test Production

Minimum required pre-production packet:

1. Concept gate: one viewer job, one mechanism, one visible first-2-second proof, one cold-viewer promise.
2. Evidence anchors: at least 3 manually verified analogs plus 2 cold/ordinary controls, with no direct copying.
3. Identity bible: persistent subject traits, props, location, continuity constraints, and rejection criteria.
4. Shot list: each shot adds new proof, contrast, action, consequence, or comment reason.
5. Risk check: policy, animal respect, copyright/music, product/claims, and conversion restraint.
6. Measurement plan: target metric by mechanism, expected failure modes, and what result would change the KB.
7. Reviewer gate: static and motion outputs pass identity and mechanism-proof review before publishing.
