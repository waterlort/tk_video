## Faceless TikTok Operating Plan

Recommended personas:

- Product Test Bench: best default. Fixed table, labels, hands/gloves, ruler/scale, one visible test per video.
- Quality Scout: use Chinese supply-chain access for inspection, rejects, materials, packaging, and "what I would test before shipping."
- Small-Space Fixer: one drawer, cabinet, table, sink, trunk, or shelf problem per episode.
- Pet-Care Desk: bench-only product tests unless real owned/licensed animal footage is available.
- Fitment Skeptic: auto interior/accessory checker with explicit fitment limits and no safety-critical repair advice.

Script formulas:

- Home: friction/result split-screen -> one product/action -> close-up proof -> soft source/test cue.
- Home comparison: two products -> one criterion -> one visible test -> ask which criterion next.
- Pet bench: product plus friction -> material/cleaning/durability proof -> size/safety boundary -> ask what pet size/use case to test.
- Pet real behavior: one real behavior -> one setup -> one action -> one payoff; no invented animal claims.
- Auto accessory: friction and installed result -> hands-only use -> limitation/fitment note -> "check your model/size."
- Auto detailing: dirty surface plus clean swipe -> process/ASMR -> before/after -> product limitation.

Recurring series:

- `Tested on the bench`
- `Would I ship this?`
- `One drawer fix`
- `Pet-safe or pass?`
- `Fitment check`
- `Under $20, but tested`
- `Comment picked the next test`
- `Factory sample vs retail version`, only when legally/factually supportable

Supply-chain bridge rules:

1. Lead with problem/result before supplier access.
2. Show use before source or shop cue.
3. Show rejects and limitations; this is the trust edge.
4. Avoid brand lookalikes, counterfeit-adjacent comparisons, hidden sponsorship, fake scarcity, or unverified origin claims.
5. For affiliate/TikTok Shop paths, use platform disclosure tools and keep claims testable.
6. Re-check TikTok Shop US product eligibility before scripting. Treat auto mechanical/repair/fitment parts, vehicle-safety items, pet health/medical/supplement claims, and restricted-category products as review-blocked until platform policy and seller eligibility are confirmed.

Low-compliance boundaries:

- Prefer: storage, organization, cleaning, washable materials, non-medical pet comfort, car interior convenience, non-critical accessories.
- Route to review: pet health/diet/anxiety/training, electrical auto accessories, tire/brake/engine/suspension/steering/airbag content, child/pet safety devices, legal/certification claims, flame resistance, counterfeit comparisons.
- Avoid: cure/guarantee language, unsafe animal handling, dangerous driving, fake testimonials, and AI/avatar product-use proof.
- First-batch rule: do not assume TikTok Shop is available for all auto-parts or pet-product ideas. Keep early posts focused on content learning unless product eligibility is separately verified.

Length and density:

- Simple product proof: 8-16 seconds.
- Comparison: 15-25 seconds.
- Utility micro-tutorial: 18-35 seconds.
- First 2 seconds must show the object/result/behavior/friction/test setup. Explanation cannot carry the opening.

Shot and asset strategy:

- Repeatable bench with inspection mat, scale, ruler, caliper, water/fur/dust props, product labels, and pass/fail cards.
- Hands-only macro footage for material, clips, hinges, seams, residue, fit, and cleaning result.
- POV drawer/cabinet/sink/trunk/dash/pet-care table shots for context.
- Captions should state test name, size/fit boundary, limitation, and disclosure when relevant.
- Visual proof must work on mute; voiceover can explain but cannot be the only proof.

## Measurement Hypotheses

- Home: saves and source comments should be the main positive signal for micro-friction fixes.
- Pet: shares/tags should rise only when real behavior is visible; bench rows should be judged more by saves, size questions, and safety objections.
- Auto: comments may signal interest or risk. Track fitment/safety objections separately from positive source comments.
- Trust-building hypothesis: showing one limitation or reject should improve comment quality versus generic "must have" pitches.
- Spam-risk hypothesis: list-style product videos without a visible test will produce weaker saves/trust than one-product proof demos.
