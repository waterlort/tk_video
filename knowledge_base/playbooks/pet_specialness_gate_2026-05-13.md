## Gate

A pet concept must pass all five checks before production handoff:

1. Persistent subject is defined.
   - Name, breed/type, age feel, color, markings, size, face shape, collar/prop, and behavior identity.
   - If there is more than one pet or person, list every persistent object and what must not change.

2. One primary extreme is selected.
   - `extreme_cute`
   - `stubborn_sassy`
   - `food_eating`
   - `surprise_reaction`
   - `care_transformation`

3. First 2 seconds contain visible proof.
   - The viewer should understand the unusual behavior without reading a long caption.
   - Text can frame the behavior, but cannot replace it.

4. The action is repeatable as a series.
   - The pet has a rule, ritual, trigger, milestone, or transformation pattern that can recur.

5. The concept still has a comment reason.
   - Viewers can answer, compare, tag, disagree, or say their pet does the same thing.

Fail condition: reject concepts that are only `normal pet + text overlay`, `cute portrait with no action`, `product first`, or `generic emotional story with no visible extreme`.

Additional rejection rules:

- reject common twists unless the specific behavior is extreme;
- reject human-like roles that do not grow from visible pet behavior;
- reject object-confusion premises when the visual logic is ambiguous;
- reject reversals that are credible but too weak;
- reject concepts that require viewers to already know the pet's training/personality;
- reject quirky habits with no stakes, chaos, obstacle, or physical consequence.

Preferred structure:

`viewer assumption -> visible pet action -> credible motive revealed -> consequence/payoff`

## Prompt Requirements For Generated Video

Every static storyboard prompt and motion prompt must include:

- the fixed pet identity block;
- the chosen specialness mechanism;
- the visible first-2-second proof;
- exact action, not just mood;
- continuity constraints for markings, collar, fur length, body size, and props;
- a no-identity-drift instruction;
- a rejection note for swapped breed, altered markings, changing pet age, or changing collar/prop.

## Mechanism Recipes

### Extreme Cute

Use when the subject has an instantly visible baby-like or tiny state.

Good proof:

- first bark,
- tiny head tilt,
- oversized paw,
- kitten or puppy fighting sleep,
- pet trying to be brave but still tiny.

Weak proof:

- pet sitting cutely,
- soft lighting only,
- caption says "cute" but nothing happens.

### Stubborn Sassy

Use when the pet enforces a rule against the human.

Good proof:

- paw grabs hand when scratching stops,
- dog refuses to move from a doorway,
- pet argues or side-eyes after a denied request,
- same ritual every night.

Weak proof:

- owner narrates "he is stubborn" without behavior proof.

### Food Eating

Use when appetite, sound, or anticipation is the spectacle.

Good proof:

- food sound instantly activates the cat,
- dog freezes then rushes to bowl,
- crunch/ASMR with clear pet reaction,
- safe treat puzzle or slow feeder.

Risk filter:

- avoid unsafe feeding, medical/diet claims, or copying raw-feeding shock details.

### Surprise Reaction

Use when a visible/audible trigger creates a fast response.

Good proof:

- orange/smell/object trigger,
- kiss avoidance,
- caught-in-the-act expression,
- new toy or sound causing a sudden pose change.

Weak proof:

- surprise explained after the fact.

### Care Transformation

Use when before/after is the spectacle.

Good proof:

- messy to fluffy,
- dull to shiny,
- muddy paws to clean paws,
- grooming shape reveal.

Weak proof:

- product shown before the pet's problem or transformation.

## Commerce Bridge Rule

For future product conversion, the order should be:

1. pet behavior proof,
2. viewer recognition or curiosity,
3. routine or transformation,
4. optional product,
5. comment-first CTA.

Do not start with product unless the goal is an ad control row.

## Judge Preflight Questions

Before production, TK Viral Judge or a reviewer should ask:

- Can the hook be predicted without seeing outcome metrics?
- Is the first-2-second proof visible?
- Is the specialness extreme enough?
- Does the concept overfit one viral row?
- Is there a cold/ordinary comparison?
- Are AI and real-pet evidence separated?
- Are all subjects identity-locked?
