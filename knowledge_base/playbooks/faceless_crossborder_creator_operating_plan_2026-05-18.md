# Faceless Cross-Border Creator Operating Plan - 2026-05-18

## Purpose

Translate the ULT-313 US creator-persona radar into a practical TikTok operating plan for a China cross-border creator who:

- cannot show a real human face;
- has strong access to Chinese quality supply chains;
- prefers low-compliance-cost content;
- wants reusable TikTok/TK traffic and later product-led conversion.

Status: operating hypothesis. Not a durable performance claim.

## Recommended Account Personas

### 1. The Product Test Bench

- Best categories: home gadgets, car accessories, pet products without medical/health claims.
- Face requirement: none.
- Visual identity: consistent table, ruler, labels, hands/gloves, macro lens, repeatable test cards.
- Trust signal: show packaging, material, stress/use test, before/after, limitations.
- Risk: can become spam if every video starts with "buy this."

### 2. The Quality Scout

- Best categories: China supply-chain comparisons, factory-side quality checks, "cheap vs premium" object tests.
- Face requirement: none.
- Visual identity: inspection mat, caliper, scale, light box, teardown tray.
- Trust signal: objective criteria, defect examples, "what I would reject."
- Risk: do not imply counterfeit/brand comparison without legal review.

### 3. The Small-Space Fixer

- Best categories: home organization, kitchen, storage, renter-friendly fixes.
- Face requirement: none.
- Visual identity: one apartment corner, drawer, cabinet, table, sink, trunk.
- Trust signal: real mess -> single fix -> visible result.
- Risk: aesthetic-only content can look like commodity dropshipping without a real problem.

### 4. The Pet-Care Desk

- Best categories: grooming tools, enrichment toys, feeding accessories, washable mats/beds.
- Face requirement: none; animal footage optional but must be ethical/owned/licensed.
- Visual identity: bench demo, pet-safe materials, size comparison, cleaning test.
- Trust signal: animal-respect boundaries, no medical claims, no forced reactions.
- Risk: pet health, diet, training, safety, and animal distress claims need review.

### 5. The Fitment Skeptic

- Best categories: non-critical car accessories, interior organizers, cleaning tools, cable clips, tire-pressure tools with disclaimers.
- Face requirement: none.
- Visual identity: hands, dashboard/trunk/interior close-ups, compatibility chart overlay.
- Trust signal: show fitment limits and vehicle context.
- Risk: safety-critical repair, road use, claims about compatibility, or misleading "universal" claims.

## Category Script Formulas

### Home / Living

Formula A: Problem -> proof -> source

1. First 2 seconds: show the mess/friction and the finished result side by side.
2. 3-8 seconds: show one product/action solving it.
3. 8-15 seconds: show close-up proof: wipe, fold, fit, no-scratch, space saved.
4. Ending: soft source cue: "Testing 3 more versions next."

Formula B: Cheap vs premium

1. First 2 seconds: show two items and the exact test.
2. Run one visible test.
3. Score one criterion only: strength, fit, residue, noise, storage space.
4. Ask which criterion to test next.

Avoid: broad room makeovers that need face/lifestyle authority, vague "must have" lists with no proof, fake scarcity.

### Pet

Formula A: Pet product desk

1. First 2 seconds: show product and one friction: fur, odor, mess, boredom, slipping.
2. 3-8 seconds: hands-only demo with material/cleaning/safety boundary.
3. 8-15 seconds: proof: washable, quiet, durable, size comparison.
4. Ending: "Would you test this with a small dog or a large dog?"

Formula B: Real behavior proof, only with ethical footage

1. First 2 seconds: show one real behavior or reaction.
2. One setup, one action, one payoff.
3. No forced fear, discomfort, unsafe feeding, or misleading health claim.
4. Use captions to clarify context, not to invent animal thoughts.

Avoid: medical promises, diet/weight claims, anxiety cure claims, dangerous toys, "AI pet story" rows as real-pet evidence.

### Auto / Accessories

Formula A: One accessory, one fitment proof

1. First 2 seconds: show the car friction and the accessory in place.
2. 3-8 seconds: hands-only install or use.
3. 8-15 seconds: close-up proof and limitation.
4. Ending: "Check your model/size before buying."

Formula B: Detailing transformation

1. First 2 seconds: dirty surface -> one clean swipe.
2. 3-12 seconds: source/ASMR action.
3. 12-20 seconds: before/after and product boundary.
4. Ending: ask for next surface to test.

Avoid: brake/engine/airbag/electrical repair advice, unsafe driving, claims that a part fits all vehicles, "factory quality" claims without proof.

## Recurring Series Ideas

- `Tested on the bench`: one product, one test, one score.
- `Would I ship this?`: inspect material, seams, packaging, defect risk.
- `One drawer fix`: one home micro-problem per episode.
- `Pet-safe or pass?`: bench-only pet product review with explicit limits.
- `Fitment check`: car accessory works/does not work in one real interior.
- `Under $20, but tested`: value test with honest pass/fail.
- `Comment picked the next test`: use comments to choose criterion, not to bait.
- `Factory sample vs retail version`: only if legally and factually supportable.

## Product / Supply-Chain Bridge Rules

1. Lead with problem/result, not supplier access.
2. Show the product in use before naming the source.
3. Use China supply-chain access as proof of testing, not as a brag.
4. Show defects and rejects; this creates trust.
5. Avoid brand lookalikes, logos, counterfeit-adjacent comparisons, and unverified origin claims.
6. For pet and auto, include fit/safety boundaries on-screen.
7. Use "I am testing" language until actual sales/returns data exists.
8. If a video has affiliate/shop links, use required disclosure tools and avoid false urgency.

## Low-Compliance Boundaries

Prefer:

- storage, organization, cleaning, non-medical pet comfort, washable materials, car interior convenience, non-critical accessories.

Route to review:

- pet health, supplements, anxiety, training corrections, raw feeding, electrical car accessories, tire/brake/engine/suspension repair, child/pet safety devices, claims about legal compliance, certifications, flame resistance, medical devices, counterfeit/brand comparisons.

Avoid:

- miracle claims, "cures," "guaranteed," fake scarcity, hidden sponsorship, unsafe animal treatment, dangerous driving, unlicensed music in commercial videos, AI/avatar pretending to have used a product.

## Video Length And Density

- Simple product proof: 8-16 seconds.
- Home/pet/auto comparison: 15-25 seconds.
- Utility micro-tutorial: 18-35 seconds.
- Avoid high-density videos until the account has proven audience retention.
- First 2 seconds must contain visible proof: product result, problem, before/after, animal behavior, car friction, or test setup.

## Shot And Asset Strategy

- Hands-only: gloves, neutral hands, sleeves, repeatable bench.
- Product macro: material, hinges, seams, clips, texture, water/fur/dust/fit.
- POV: drawer/cabinet/trunk/dash/pet-care table.
- Teardown table: components and defect markers.
- Screen overlays: test name, score, size, limitation, "not a safety part."
- UGC-style object demo: phone handheld, no polished ad voice.
- Voiceover: useful for explanation, but visual proof must work on mute.
- Captions: short, concrete, safe-zone aware.
- AI/avatar: use only as a narrator label or diagram, never as a fake user testimonial or fake product-use proof.

## Measurement Hypotheses

- Home: saves and source comments should outperform broad shares if the product solves a clear micro-friction.
- Pet: shares/tags rise when real behavior is visible; saves rise on care/tool rows.
- Auto: comments should cluster around fitment, safety, and disagreement. High comment volume can be risk, not just success.
- Bench-test persona should produce more trust comments than generic "Amazon finds" lists.
- Showing one limitation should reduce spam perception and improve comment quality.

## Anti-Overfit

Scout-only evidence:

- Secondary creator rankings.
- Public TikTok tag/discover rows.
- Facebook metrics from non-direct sources.
- Any row without manual first-3-second playback.

Verified evidence in this pass:

- Public source URLs and local JSON files exist.
- Some rows expose public view/like/comment/share/collect proxies.
- Platform policy sources support compliance boundaries.

Missing manual review:

- first 3 seconds;
- shot order;
- audio role;
- comments;
- source originality;
- direct current platform metrics;
- conversion results.

What must not become durable claims:

- "Home/pet/auto are proven winner niches."
- "Follower size on Instagram/Facebook predicts TikTok success."
- "Any China-sourced gadget can convert if demoed."
- "Auto accessories are low-risk by default."
- "Pet content works without real behavior or fixed subject identity."
