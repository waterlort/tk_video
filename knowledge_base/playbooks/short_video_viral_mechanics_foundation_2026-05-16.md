# Short-Video Viral Mechanics Foundation

## Purpose

This playbook translates the 2026-05-16 cross-vertical scout runs into a practical analysis and production framework. It is meant to build reusable cognition, not to copy any candidate video.

Use this before:

- analyzing a reference video,
- writing a script,
- planning a storyboard,
- generating static images or motion prompts,
- deciding whether a concept is strong enough to test.

## Core Rule

A candidate idea is not ready until the first 2 seconds contain visible proof of the video's promise.

The promise can be:

- a joke behavior,
- a reaction,
- a result,
- a before/after contrast,
- a comment/artifact,
- a product use case,
- a social claim,
- a process state change.

Topic heat alone is not enough.

## Required Analysis Fields

| Field | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Content category | What viewer job does it serve: laugh, save, argue, buy, identify, follow? |
| Plot setting | What situation does the viewer understand without explanation? |
| Viewer tension | What question, conflict, promise, or curiosity gap keeps attention? |
| First 2 seconds | What visible proof appears before explanation? |
| Hook type | Result first, problem first, POV, comment reply, wait-for-it, ranking, red flag, transformation, product demo, story tease. |
| Script structure | What is the beat order? |
| Shot sequence | What does each shot add that was not already known? |
| Camera method | Static, handheld, close-up, POV, screen recording, split-screen, green screen, product close-up, timelapse. |
| Editing rhythm | Single-shot tension, fast cuts, progressive reveal, beat sync, caption-led, loop ending. |
| Audio role | Original dialogue, voiceover, music bed, beat driver, SFX, ASMR/source sound, silence/pause. |
| Text role | Context, punchline, steps, labels, proof, CTA, captions for silent viewing. |
| Information density | How much new information per second? Is it visual, verbal, or text-based? |
| Understanding difficulty | Can a cold viewer understand without prior account lore? |
| Duration logic | Is the video short because the payoff is simple, or longer because each beat raises value? |
| Comment/share/save path | What action does the viewer naturally want to take? |

## Viewer Job Model

Classify by viewer job before surface topic.

| Viewer job | Strong mechanics | Weak failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Laugh/share | POV, skit, prank, reaction, role conflict | Caption-only joke with no behavior proof |
| Identify/tag | worktok, parenting, dating, couple, teacher, customer roles | Generic "relatable" label without specific behavior |
| Save/apply | tutorial, AI/tool demo, study, recipe, cleaning, marketing tips | Too many steps before a result |
| Argue/comment | hot take, red flag, ranking, debate prompt | Vague bait with no concrete example |
| Buy/want | product demo, before/after, use case, texture, result | Product shown before need/result is clear |
| Follow/series | recurring character, house rule, repeated format, comment prompt | One-off premise with no repeatable constraint |

## Script Templates

### Reaction / Wait-For-It

Beat order:

1. Show trigger or setup.
2. Hold just long enough for expectation.
3. Show reaction/payoff.
4. Hold or replay expression.
5. Optional caption prompt.

Target duration: 6-12s.

Best when the trigger is visible, payoff is fast, and the viewer can understand without caption.

### Relatable POV / Skit

Beat order:

1. Name the role or situation.
2. Show the behavior.
3. Escalate or reveal the contrast.
4. Punchline or reaction.
5. Tag/share prompt only if natural.

Target duration: 8-20s.

Best when the role is common, the behavior is specific, and the joke is acted rather than explained.

### Comment Reply / Green Screen

Beat order:

1. Show comment/artifact.
2. State or imply the challenge.
3. Answer, perform, prove, or rank.
4. Invite next comment only after payoff.

Target duration: 10-25s.

Best when the comment is readable in the first second and the response adds something new.

### Utility Micro-Tutorial

Beat order:

1. Promise result.
2. Show final or example quickly.
3. Give 2-4 steps.
4. Show proof/result.
5. Save/follow CTA.

Target duration: 18-35s.

Best when steps are visually chunked, the result is concrete, and text overlays reduce cognitive load.

### Product / Commerce Demo

Beat order:

1. Show result, problem, or unusual object.
2. Show product in use.
3. Prove one or two key benefits.
4. Address friction or context.
5. Soft source CTA if comments/product fit exists.

Target duration: 12-25s.

Best when product use is self-explanatory, close-ups prove features, and selling starts after curiosity or need.

### Transformation / Process

Beat order:

1. Before state or final teaser.
2. Key process steps.
3. Texture/sensory proof.
4. Reveal.
5. Save/share prompt.

Target duration: 18-45s.

Best when every shot changes the state, sound supports texture, and the viewer can imagine repeating it.

### Debate / Hot Take / Ranking

Beat order:

1. Specific claim or ranking frame.
2. One concrete example.
3. Reason or boundary.
4. Ask for agreement, counterexample, or ranking.

Target duration: 15-35s.

Best when the claim is specific, the viewer has a clear position to take, and the video avoids empty bait.

## Shot Planning Rules

Every shot must add at least one of:

- new context,
- new proof,
- new emotion,
- new contrast,
- new step,
- new consequence,
- new reason to comment/save/share.

Reject shots that only repeat the same information.

## Camera And Editing Patterns

| Mechanic | Camera | Editing | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reaction/prank | handheld or static, close enough for face/body | single-shot tension or quick reveal | Trust and readability. |
| POV/skit | locked phone or handheld room shot | role label, cut to reaction, punchline hold | Cold-viewer role clarity. |
| Utility/tool | screen recording, face insert, product/desktop close-up | caption-led steps | Saves cognitive effort. |
| Product demo | hands, product close-up, use context | result-first, feature cuts | Proof before selling. |
| Transformation | before/after wide plus texture close-ups | montage and reveal | Shows state change. |
| Debate/ranking | face-to-camera or green-screen artifact | claim card, examples, list cuts | Keeps argument legible. |

## Audio And SFX Rules

- Use original speech when social behavior, role conflict, or reaction is the value.
- Use voiceover when the viewer must understand steps, claims, or story compression.
- Use music as emotional pacing, not as a substitute for proof.
- Use SFX only to mark state changes, reveals, score updates, or comedy beats.
- Use source/ASMR sound when texture is the product: cleaning, cooking, grooming, unboxing, tapping, crunching.
- Add captions when spoken content carries the hook; many viewers watch silently.

## Information Density

### Low Density

Best for reaction, visual jokes, pet/child behavior, pranks, and simple product results.

Rule: one setup and one payoff.

### Medium Density

Best for POV skits, work/relationship/parenting role conflict, product demos, rankings, and routines.

Rule: one situation, 2-4 beats, no extra lore.

### High Density

Best for AI/tool tutorials, marketing tips, recipes, study systems, and side-hustle breakdowns.

Rule: simple promise first; then chunk the information visually.

## Understanding Difficulty Gate

Reject or rewrite if a cold viewer needs:

- prior account lore,
- hidden creator context,
- more than one unfamiliar term before payoff,
- a long caption to understand the first shot,
- external knowledge that the average target audience may not have.

Difficulty can be high only after the first promise is easy.

## Concept Preflight Checklist

Before production, answer:

- What is the viewer job?
- What is visible in the first 2 seconds?
- What is the exact tension or promise?
- What beat order does the script use?
- What proof appears before explanation?
- What shot would a viewer rewatch, save, share, or argue with?
- What audio role is necessary?
- What text must be readable on mute?
- What is the target duration band and why?
- What is the risk: policy, overclaim, copyright, identity drift, off-topic trend copying?

## Production Test Matrix

| Slot | Mechanic | Metric to watch |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-2 | Relatable role skit | shares, tags, retention to punchline |
| 3-4 | Reaction/wait-for-it | retention to payoff, rewatch, shares |
| 5-6 | Comment reply | comments requesting next, follows |
| 7-8 | Utility micro-tutorial | saves, follows, completion |
| 9-10 | Product/result demo | saves, source questions, profile visits |
| 11-12 | Debate/ranking | comments, reply chains, shares |

Do not promote a durable claim until each mechanic has mixed-outcome examples.
